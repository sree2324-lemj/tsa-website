Fred G. Bond Park

Bond Park is one of the largest parks in Cary, spanning about 310 acres. The park was named after Fred G. Bond, a Cary Mayor who helped make the vision of a public park a reality. The park contains a mix of woody trails, open fields, and waterfront areas all centered around Bond Lake. People who visit Bond Park can do a variety of activities, including hiking, walking, and biking.

Bond Park also contains a range of recreational opportunities like picnicking, athletic fields, and even playgrounds, which create a great experience for both chill families and athletic visitors. The Park even has a boathouse called the Bond Park Boathouse where they offer boat rentals and lakeside amenities, encouraging water based activitys like paddling and fishing.
