Fred G. Bond Park

Bond Park is one of the largest parks in Cary, spanning about 310 acres. The town named the park after Fred G. Bond, a Cary mayor who helped make the vision of a public park a reality. The park contains a mix of wooded trails, open fields, and waterfront areas all centered around Bond Lake. People who visit Bond Park can do a variety of activities, including hiking, walking, and biking.

Bond Park also contains a range of recreational opportunities like picnicking, athletic fields, and even playgrounds, which create a great experience for both chill families and athletic visitors. The park features the Bond Park Boathouse. Here, you can rent boats and enjoy lakeside amenities. This encourages fun activities like paddling and fishing.
