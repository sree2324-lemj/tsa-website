Cary Lions Club

The Cary Lions Club is a nonprofit volunteer organization that was founded on September 4, 1956, making it one of the oldest community organizations in Cary. It operates as part of Lions Club International, which is the largest service organization in the world. They meet every first and third Monday of the month at the First United Methodist Church at 175 Academy Street in downtown Cary. The club's main philosophy is known as Lionism, which empowers volunteers to serve their community starting locally and extending to the state, country, and world. Melvin Jones started this philosophy. It suggests you won't go far until you help others, not just yourself. For decades, the Cary Lions Club has embraced this idea.

The Cary Lions Club carries out its mission through many different forms of community service, including projects and fundraisers. One of its most well-known initiatives was when it made an eyeglasses collection program to help fight blindness and vision impairment in the community. The club also raises funds to support local projects, and it also actively recruits passionate volunteers who bring fresh ideas to the table. Through consistent dedication, the Cary Lions Club remains a vital part of our society.
