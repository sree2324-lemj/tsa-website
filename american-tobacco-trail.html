American Tobacco Trail

The American Tobacco Trail, also known as ATT, is a trail that spans 22.6 miles and is located in the Research Triangle area of North Carolina. The trail was originally made in 1906 to be a railroad corridor and transport tobacco from local farming communities; however, over time, the railroad was abandoned, and the corridor was transformed into the greenway that it is today. Stretching through Durham, Chatham, and Wake Counties, the Tobacco Trail is a common hotspot for walkers, bikers, and other non-motorized units.

The American Tobacco Trail gives a diverse experience for people who enjoy the outdoors. It has a beautiful end made out of crushed stone and natural surfaces that many people really enjoy seeing. Along the trail, people can cross streams, wetlands, and also watch wildlife like beavers, herons, turtles, and deer, making it one of the most enjoyable places if you really enjoy biking, walking, or just being outdoors in general.
