North Carolina Wildlife Resources Commision

The NC Wildlife Resources Commission, also known as NCWRC, is a state government agency created by the General Assembly in 1947. The organization was created to conserve and sustain wildlife and fish populations across North Carolina. The NCWRC successfully does these things by researching, scientific management, wise use, and public input. The agency operates under a commission governance model made up of 19 members who serve as trustees for the fish and wildlife resources of NC.
