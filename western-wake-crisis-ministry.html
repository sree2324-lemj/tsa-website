Western Wake Crisis Ministry

Western Wake Crisis Ministry (WWCM) is a nonprofit faith-based organization founded by Antoinette Clark in 1983. Antoinette Clark realized that the residents of Western Wake County needed assistance. What started in the basement of a local Baptist church, only serving seven families per week, has grown into an important community resource that serves 200 families per month. Operating out of 1600 Olive Chapel Road, this organization has spent over 4 decades helping many residents across Apex, Cary, Fuquay-Varina, and Holly Springs. In 2025, WWCM merged with Dorcas Ministries to create a new organization called NeighborUP. They combined their similar missions to make a bigger impact and help more residents across the community.

Western Wake Crisis Ministry has a wide range of services to help individuals with financial hardships and crisis situations. The food pantry allows residents to choose their own items. Although a home delivery option is also accessible to seniors, the disabled, and the elderly who can't make it in person. The organization also helps with emergency financial assistance for rent, utilities, and medications, while a Workforce Development program assists clients by pairing them with mentors who help them get ready for a job by building skills, making resumes, and finding employment opportunities. The organization is funded through community donations and revenue from its thrift shops. WWCM has been committed to helping its neighbors, creating support during crisis, and building a path toward long-term stability.
