Meals On Wheels Wake County

When ten volunteers gathered at Hillyer Christian Church to deliver meals to residents that were homebound in downtown Raleigh, Meals on Wheels, a nonprofit organization, was founded on February 12th, 1974. Over the next five decades, the organization has grown to now serve 1,300 noontime meals every weekday to older adults and the disabled across Wake County. They are located at 1001 Blair Drive in Raleigh, now a part of Meals on Wheels America, which is a nationwide network that serves 5,000 senior nutrition programs with 800,000 volunteers helping across the country. Since the founding of this organization, they have served over nine million meals in Wake County alone, making this one of the most impactful senior service organizations in the region. 

Meals on Wheels of Wake County serves residents who are 60+ years of age and cannot leave their residence without help, making them homebound. Volunteers deliver them hot meals Monday through Friday from 10:00 am to 12:30 pm. Each route consists of about 8-12 stops, taking about 60-90 minutes to finish. They also provide salt check-ins and friendly visits, which may be the only human interactions that the recipients have throughout the entire day. They also provide frozen food to residents in isolated areas, communal dining for adults 60+ in age, and a Hospital at Home program that gives recently discharged patients who are at risk hot meals for ten days after their discharge.
