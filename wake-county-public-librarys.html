Wake County Public Librarys

Wake County Public Libraries is a public library system serving the residents of Wake County. There are many libraries in this area; however, their administration is headquartered at 4020 Carya Drive in Raleigh. The system has a very rich history dating back over 100 years to 1901, eventually growing into one of the largest library systems in the state, serving over 920,000 people. Wake County Public Libraries' mission is to instill a love of reading and to foster the pursuit of knowledge for everyone in Wake County.

Wake County Public Libraries has over 23 different locations across the county, ranging from large regional libraries to small bookmobiles that bring library service to the residents. Beyond physical books, the library system also provides access to digital books and audiobooks. The library system strives to maintain a welcoming environment for anyone who wants to read.
